## Use Non-Privileged Accounts or Roles for Non-Administrative Functions

---

## Control Overview

This document identifies evidence used to demonstrate implementation of
AC.L2-3.1.6, which requires the use of non-privileged accounts or roles
for non-administrative functions within the CMMC enclave.

This evidence supports the control response documented in the System Security Plan (SSP).

---

## Evidence Objectives

Evidence for this control demonstrates that:

- Users perform routine activities using non-privileged accounts
- Privileged accounts are restricted to administrative or security functions
- Separate accounts or roles exist for privileged and non-privileged use
- Privileged account usage is monitored and reviewed

---

## Evidence Artifacts

### 1. Account Separation

Evidence demonstrating separation of privileged and non-privileged accounts may include:

- User account listings showing standard and administrative accounts
- Documentation mapping privileged accounts to assigned individuals
- System configuration showing administrative roles not assigned to standard users

---

### 2. Privileged Account Restrictions

Evidence demonstrating restricted use of privileged accounts may include:

- Role or group membership listings for administrative access
- System settings preventing standard users from executing privileged functions
- Configuration records limiting privileged role assignment

---

### 3. Privileged Account Usage Monitoring

Evidence demonstrating oversight of privileged account usage may include:

- Authentication or access logs showing privileged account activity
- Audit logs of administrative actions
- Records of management review of privileged activity

---

### 4. User Training and Awareness

Evidence demonstrating user understanding of account usage requirements may include:

- Security awareness or role-based training records
- User acknowledgments regarding proper account usage
- Training materials covering privileged vs. non-privileged access

---

## Evidence Retention

Evidence supporting this control is retained in accordance with organizational
policy and contractual requirements and is available for review during assessment.

---

## Notes

This document identifies representative evidence artifacts.
Organizations may implement different technical or procedural controls provided
the same objectives are met and evidence is available.
