## Employ the Principle of Least Privilege

---

## Control Overview

This document identifies evidence used to demonstrate implementation of
AC.L2-3.1.5, which requires the organization to employ the principle of least
privilege for users, processes, and privileged accounts.

This evidence supports the control response documented in the System Security Plan (SSP).

---

## Evidence Objectives

Evidence for this control demonstrates that:

- Access privileges are limited to the minimum necessary
- Privileged access is restricted and justified
- Elevated permissions are controlled and reviewed
- Excess or unnecessary permissions are removed

---

## Evidence Artifacts

### 1. Role-Based Access Definitions

Evidence demonstrating least privilege design may include:

- Role definitions mapping job functions to permissions
- Access control policy defining privilege levels
- Role-to-permission matrices

---

### 2. User and Privileged Account Permissions

Evidence demonstrating enforcement of least privilege may include:

- User permission listings by role
- Privileged account inventories
- System or application permission configuration records

---

### 3. Privileged Access Approvals

Evidence demonstrating controlled privileged access may include:

- Approval records for elevated access
- Documentation justifying privileged role assignments
- Records showing separation between standard and privileged accounts

---

### 4. Access Reviews and Adjustments

Evidence demonstrating ongoing enforcement may include:

- Periodic access review records
- Documentation of permission changes or removals
- Records showing revocation of access after role changes or termination

---

## Evidence Retention

Evidence supporting this control is retained in accordance with organizational
policy and contractual requirements and is available for review during assessment.

---

## Notes

This document identifies representative evidence artifacts.
Organizations may implement different technical or procedural controls provided
the same objectives are met and evidence is available.
